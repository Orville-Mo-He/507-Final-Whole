We Sent Love Through Our Thumbs



 In late  2020, I    got     very  sick.  After many  months,  specialists  and
  dollars,   there  is still only
  a   guess:  long-haul  Covid.    I    couldn’t breathe properly.     An alien lived    inside
 me. I    was single,     scared. What  if  something   happened  to   me?  My   friend  asked  me  daily  for   my  “thumb
 status,”
   even  though he hates   texting    because   he   says  his  thumbs  are
  too  big.   On   rare   good   days,    I
 sent  photos  with     a
  thumbs   up.   More
    were    neutral   thumbs.
  Most   were thumbs    down.     Pain.  Fatigue.   Fear.   Every  day  he
 asked.    Every   day  I answered.  Every   day    we  sent  love   through     our  thumbs. —  Emily O.  Gravett
